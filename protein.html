Welcome to your guide for fueling your body with protein for calisthenics! Calisthenics relies on building strength and control using only your body weight, and proper nutrition especially protein is essential for making progress. This guide will help you choose the right foods, know how much you need, and find the perfect time to fuel your body for optimal performance and recovery.
How Much Protein Do You Need?
As a calisthenics athlete, your protein needs are higher than the average person. Proper intake is crucial for muscle repair and building the strength to progress with skills like pull-ups, muscle-ups, and planches.
Example: If you weigh 70kg (about 154 lbs), you should target around 84 to 140 grams of protein per day, depending on your training intensity

Breakfast is a great start: Beginning your day with protein helps you feel full and provides the energy you need for a training session later on

Post-Workout: After a calisthenics workout, your muscles need to repair. Consuming a protein-rich snack or shake within a couple of hours helps with muscle recovery and growth.

Spread it out: The most effective approach is to spread your protein intake across all your meals and snacks to ensure a steady supply of amino acids throughout the day.
